Space Groove

Our Story

In the pursuit of its mission objectives in aeronautics and Space Administration, Space Groove continues pushing the boundaries of the future. Often, new technologies are needed to either enhance our current capabilities or enable future ones. The Intergration Partner Program (IPP) provides the organizational structure for acquiring, maturing, technology and capabilities for Space Groove's Mission Programs and Projects throughpartnerships with Industry, Academia, Government companies. As one of Space Groove's Mission Support Offices, the IPP supports all Mission Directorates. In addition to leveraged technology changes, dual-use technology-related partnerships, and technology solutions accelerates technology maturation.

Responsibility Duties
The HRIRB and the OPRP are responsible for ensuring the rights and well-being of human subjects and research participants in all Ames aeronautics and space flight investigations, where NASA Ames investigators are involved, whether as principal or co-investigators or NASA Ames points of contact, and whether the research is conducted at Ames or at other sites off campus. On an annual basis, NASA is required by law to develop a budget, and report to Congress and the public on its fiscal management and programmatic performance. The NASA Strategic Plan is updated every four years to outline the strategic direction, goals, and priorities the Agency will pursue to achieve our Mission.
